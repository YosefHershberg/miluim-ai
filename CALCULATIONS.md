# IDF Reservist Compensation — תגמולי מילואים
## Complete Calculation Reference
> Last researched: March 24, 2026 | Sources: btl.gov.il, kolzchut.org.il, gov.il, miluim.idf.il, news sources
> **Rates shown are as of January 1, 2026 unless otherwise noted. Wartime provisions relate to Operation Swords of Iron (חרבות ברזל) and the war against Iran (מלחמת התקומה).**

---

## 1. Legal Framework

### Key Laws

- **חוק הביטוח הלאומי [נוסח משולב], התשנ"ה-1995** — National Insurance Law (consolidated version). Governs the core reservist compensation (תגמול מילואים) paid by the National Insurance Institute (NII / ביטוח לאומי). Defines calculation methodology, minimum/maximum rates, and employer reimbursement.
- **חוק שירות המילואים, התשס"ח-2008** — Reserve Service Law. Establishes the framework for reserve duty, annual service caps, and the "Additional Compensation" (תגמול נוסף) and "Special Compensation" (תגמול מיוחד) paid based on annual cumulative service days (Section 19).
- **פקודת מס הכנסה [נוסח חדש]** — Income Tax Ordinance. Amendment No. 283 (2025) introduced tax credit points (נקודות זיכוי) for combat reservists starting from tax year 2026.
- **חוק הביטוח הלאומי (תיקון מס' 253 והוראת שעה), התשפ"ה-2025** — Published January 21, 2025. Changed compensation calculation for reservists called to additional service from May 1, 2025 onward (within 90 days of previous service), to prevent "snowball effect" of inflated compensation.
- **Government Decisions (החלטות ממשלה)**: Decision No. 2540 (Dec 9, 2024) and subsequent decisions in 2025 — established wartime grant frameworks, combat differentiation, family grants, and employer incentives.

### Who Administers What

| Payment Type | Administered By |
|---|---|
| Core reservist compensation (תגמול מילואים) | National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) |
| Additional Compensation (תגמול נוסף) | IDF via Tax Authority (רשות המסים) — paid in May |
| Special Compensation (תגמול מיוחד) | IDF via MOFET (מופ"ת) — paid in May |
| Wartime grants & family grants | Ministry of Defense / IDF via MOFET (מופ"ת) |
| Business owner compensation for income loss | Tax Authority (רשות המסים) |
| Employer social contribution reimbursement | National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) |
| Tax credit points for combat reservists | Tax Authority — via payroll (Form 101) |
| Aid Fund grants (קרן הסיוע) | Ministry of Defense — via Miluim 360 Center |

### Regular Service vs. Wartime Emergency (צו 8)

- **Regular service (שגרה)**: Governed by standard NII rules. Minimum compensation was historically lower (222.27 NIS/day in 2024).
- **Emergency Order / צו 8**: During wartime, special provisions apply — elevated minimum rates, additional grants for 60+ day service, combat differentials, and family support grants. Most wartime provisions since Oct 7, 2023 remain active through 2025.

## 3. Detailed Calculation Formulas

### 3.1 Core Reservist Compensation (תגמול מילואים)

**Eligibility:** All persons called to active reserve duty (שמ"פ), including emergency orders (צו 8), single-day and half-day service, and civil defense training (מל"ח).

**Paid by:** National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי). For salaried employees, paid via the employer who is then reimbursed. For self-employed and others, paid directly.

**Formula:**

```
Daily_Rate = (Gross salary in 3 months preceding service) ÷ 90
Total_Compensation = Daily_Rate × Adjusted_Service_Days
```

The total compensation includes an automatic 40% supplement (built into the calculation methodology).

**Variables:**
- **Gross salary (שכר ברוטו):** The gross salary subject to NII contributions in the 3 months before the month service began. Includes sick pay, maternity pay, vacation pay, previous reservist compensation, unemployment benefits, and work injury compensation.
- **Adjusted service days:** Service days are divided by 7. For full weeks (multiples of 7), each day counts as 1. For the remainder, a conversion table applies:

| Remainder days | Counted as |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1.4 days |
| 2 | 2.4 days |
| 3 | 4.2 days |
| 4 | 4.2 days |
| 5 | 7.0 days |
| 6 | 7.0 days |

**Current Rates (NIS) — as of January 1, 2026:**
- Minimum: **328.76 NIS/day** | **9,863 NIS/month**
- Maximum: **1,730.33 NIS/day** | **51,910 NIS/month**
- Half-day service: 50% of daily rate
- Young worker (נער עובד) minimum: 114.73 NIS/day

**Basic Amount (סכום בסיסי):** 10,382 NIS/month (as of 01.01.2026). Maximum = 5× basic amount. Minimum = 95% of basic amount.

**Worked Example — Salaried Employee:**
> Employee earns 15,000 NIS/month gross, serves 30 days:
> - Step 1: Total gross for 3 months = 15,000 × 3 = 45,000 NIS
> - Step 2: Daily rate = 45,000 ÷ 90 = 500 NIS/day
> - Step 3: Check min/max: 500 > 328.76 (min) and 500 < 1,730.33 (max) ✓
> - Step 4: Adjusted days for 30 days = 28 full weeks (4 × 7) + remainder 2 = 28 + 2.4 = 30.4 days
> - Step 5: Total = 500 × 30.4 = **15,200 NIS**

**Worked Example — Minimum Wage Earner:**
> Employee earns 6,247.67 NIS/month, serves 30 days:
> - Daily rate = (6,247.67 × 3) ÷ 90 = 208.26 NIS/day
> - 208.26 < minimum of 328.76, so **minimum applies**: 328.76 NIS/day
> - Total = 328.76 × 30.4 = **9,994.30 NIS** (≈ 9,863 NIS/month minimum)

**Wartime Updates (post Oct 7, 2023):**
- During the Swords of Iron war period, the minimum was elevated to approximately 310.52 NIS/day (9,316 NIS/month) for 2024. From 2025 the minimum was raised further under new legislation, reaching 321.07 NIS/day (9,632 NIS/month) for 2025 and 328.76 NIS/day (9,863 NIS/month) for 2026.
- **Amendment 253 (effective May 1, 2025):** For reservists called to new service within 90 days of previous service: if current income is lower than previous period income, compensation is based on the higher previous income (benefiting the reservist). If current income is more than 20% higher than previous income, compensation is capped at previous income + 20%. If the salary increase is due to work only (not previous reservist payments), the employer can request the difference from NII.

### 3.2 Self-Employed Compensation (עצמאי)

**Eligibility:** Self-employed workers registered with NII.

**Paid by:** NII — automatically if NII contributions are current; otherwise via personal claim.

**Formula:**

```
Daily_Rate = (Annual gross income ÷ 12 × 3 months) ÷ 90
            = Annual gross income ÷ 360

Initially based on advance payments (מקדמות).
Final adjustment after annual tax assessment (שומה).
```

For self-employed, the income used for the calculation includes a 25% supplement on the income subject to NII contributions.

**Special wartime rule (2023–2025):** Compensation is calculated based on the highest of: advance payments reported to NII by Oct 31, 2024; final assessment for 2024; or final assessment for 2025.

**Worked Example — Self-Employed:**
> Annual gross income: 180,000 NIS. Serves 20 days.
> - Monthly equivalent: 180,000 ÷ 12 = 15,000 NIS
> - 3-month total: 15,000 × 3 = 45,000 NIS
> - Daily rate: 45,000 ÷ 90 = 500 NIS/day (before 25% supplement on NII-subject income)
> - Adjusted days for 20 days: 14 (2×7) + remainder 6 → 14 + 7 = 21 adjusted days
> - Total: 500 × 21 = **10,500 NIS**
>
> ⚠️ Note: After final tax assessment, NII will recalculate. If actual income was higher, the reservist receives the difference + CPI adjustment. If lower, the reservist must repay.

### 3.3 Additional Compensation (תגמול נוסף)

**Eligibility:** Reservists who performed 10 or more cumulative active reserve days in a calendar year.

**Paid by:** IDF / Tax Authority (רשות המסים), paid in May of the following year.

**Formula:**

```
Amount = Credit_Point_Value × Multiplier (based on days served)
```

Multiplier is based on service days per calendar year, calculated as fractions of a tax credit point (נקודת זיכוי). The maximum is 2 credit points.

**Credit Point Value (2026):** 2,904 NIS/year = 242 NIS/month.

| Days Served (cumulative/year) | Multiplier (credit points) | Gross Amount (NIS) |
|---|---|---|
| 10–31 | Graduated scale | Up to ~1 credit point |
| 32+ | Up to 2 credit points max | Up to ~5,808 NIS |

⚠️ Exact per-day table varies by year; check the IDF Miluim website for current tables. The Additional Compensation is subject to a special 25% income tax rate — no deductions, credits, or exemptions apply.

**Tax status:** Not considered income for NII or health insurance purposes. Subject only to the special 25% tax.

---

### 3.4 Special Compensation (תגמול מיוחד)

**Eligibility:** Reservists who performed 32–60 cumulative days of active reserve in a calendar year. During wartime (צו 8), also applies beyond day 60.

**Paid by:** IDF via MOFET, paid by May 1 of the following year.

**Formula:**

```
Amount = (Eligible_Days) × 133.33 NIS/day
```

Where Eligible_Days = days 32 through 60 (max 29 days), plus days beyond 60 under צו 8.

**Worked Example:**
> Reservist served 50 days total in 2025:
> - Eligible days for Special Compensation: 50 − 31 = 19 days
> - Amount: 19 × 133.33 = **2,533.27 NIS**

---

### 3.5 Enhanced Personal Expenses Grant (מענק הוצאות אישיות מוגדל)

**Eligibility:** Reservists who served 40+ days under צו 8 in the Swords of Iron war / war against Iran. Paid from day 40 onward.

**Paid by:** Ministry of Defense / IDF.

**Formula (per 10 service days from day 40):**

| Role | Amount per 10 days |
|---|---|
| General reservist (all units) | 266 NIS |
| Combat unit (מערך לוחם) | 466 NIS |
| Territorial Defense (הגמ"ר) — from Jan 1, 2025 | 310 NIS |

**Worked Example:**
> Combat reservist served 80 days under צו 8:
> - Eligible from day 40: 80 − 40 = 40 days = 4 blocks of 10 days
> - Amount: 4 × 466 = **1,864 NIS**

### 3.6 Family Grants (מענקי משפחה)

**Basic Family Grant (מענק משפחה):**
- Eligibility: 8+ days under צו 8, with a child under age 14
- Amount: Fixed grant (varies by year; check IDF Miluim site)

**Enhanced Family Grant (מענק משפחה מוגדל):**
- Eligibility: 40+ days under צו 8, with a child under age 14
- Per 10 service days:

| Role | Amount per 10 days |
|---|---|
| General reservist | 500 NIS |
| Combat unit (מערך לוחם) | 833 NIS |
| Territorial Defense (הגמ"ר) — from Jan 1, 2025 | 600 NIS |

**Special Family Grant (מענק משפחה מיוחדת):**
- For reservists in 2024 who served 45+ days under צו 8 with a child with disabilities (age 3–21): additional 2,000 NIS.

### 3.7 Household Maintenance Grant (מענק כלכלת הבית)

**Eligibility:** Recognized active reservist (משרת מילואים פעיל).

**Amount:** 1,250 NIS/month paid at specified dates (previously 620 NIS under earlier government decision, now combined to 1,250 NIS).

**For combat reservists who served 45+ days in 2025:** Enhanced to 2,500 NIS.

### 3.8 Persistence Grant (מענק התמדה) — 2025

**Eligibility:** Reservists performing 10+ days of non-צו 8 service in 2025 (after the order expires), who had previously served 60+ days under צו 8 in 2023–2025.

**Amount:** 100 NIS/day, from day 10 onward.

**Paid by:** MOFET, to the IDF-registered bank account.

**Paid by:** Applied as tax credit in payroll (Form 101) in the following tax year.

| 75–79 | 2.25 | 6,534 |
| 80–84 | 2.50 | 7,260 |
| 85–89 | 2.75 | 7,986 |

**Credit point value (2026):** 2,904 NIS/year. This reduces income tax owed, but cannot generate a refund beyond total tax liability.

### 3.11 Digital Wallet Grant (מענק ארנק דיגיטלי) — 2025

**Eligibility:** Combat reservists, 10+ days in 2025.

**Formula:**
```
Days 10–30: 30 NIS/day
Day 31+: 80 NIS/day
Maximum: 5,000 NIS total
```

To reach the maximum of 5,000 NIS requires approximately 80 days of service. The grant is loaded onto a digital wallet for government fees, recreation, and welfare purposes.

---

### 3.12 Business Owner Compensation (פיצוי לבעלי עסקים)

**Eligibility:** Self-employed or controlling shareholders (25%+ ownership) called under צו 8 for 21+ days (Track 2) or 30+ days (Track 1), whose business income declined.

**Paid by:** Tax Authority (רשות המסים).

**Formula:** Based on comparison of business turnover (מחזור עסקאות) in the claim period vs. equivalent periods in 2022–2023. Two tracks available for different periods in 2024.

**Supplementary aid:** Available through the Reservist Aid Fund (קרן הסיוע) for amounts beyond the Tax Authority compensation, up to the maximum daily reservist compensation rate of 1,689.83 NIS/day or 50,695 NIS/month (2025 figures).

⚠️ Complex calculation — consult a tax professional or use the Tax Authority online tools.

---

### 3.13 Employer Social Contribution Reimbursement (שיפוי מעסיקים)

**Eligibility:** Non-public-sector employers (not government ministries, public corporations, or government companies) whose employees served in reserves.

**Formula:**

```
Daily_Reimbursement = 20% × (Employee's 3-month gross salary ÷ 90)
Total_Reimbursement = Daily_Reimbursement × Service_Days
```

**Purpose:** Compensates employers for social contributions (pension, training fund, NII payments) they continued paying during employee's reserve service.

**Status:** Established by collective agreement (April 2024) and NII regulations. ⚠️ As of research date, these regulations were temporary through end of 2025; extension or permanent legislation pending.

## 4. Special Cases

### 4.1 Salaried Employee (שכיר)

**Standard flow:**
1. Employee is called to reserve duty.
2. Employer continues paying regular salary as if the employee were working.
3. Employer submits claim to NII (Form 501 / online WCF system) for reimbursement.
4. NII calculates compensation based on 3-month gross salary ÷ 90.
5. If NII's calculated compensation exceeds the salary paid, the employer must pass the difference to the employee.
6. Deductions from compensation: NII contributions, health insurance, income tax, pension/provident fund contributions.

**Employee who worked less than 60 days in 3 months before service:** Compensation calculated based on the 3 months with highest income out of the 6 months preceding service.

**Employee with multiple employers:** Main employer pays and claims from NII. Employee files personal claim (Form 502) for additional income from other employers, attaching 6 months of pay stubs from all workplaces. Total cannot exceed maximum daily rate.

**New employee (just started):** Submit 6 pay stubs from previous employer. If no previous employment in 6 months, minimum rate applies.

---

### 4.2 Self-Employed (עצמאי)

1. NII pays compensation automatically if advance payments (מקדמות) are current.
2. Initial calculation based on advance payment amounts.
3. After final tax assessment (שומה), NII recalculates.
4. If assessment shows higher income → reservist receives the difference + CPI adjustment.
5. If assessment shows lower income → reservist must repay the difference.
6. For 2023–2025 (wartime): calculation uses the highest of advance payments, 2024 assessment, or 2025 assessment.

**Deductions:** NII and health insurance contributions are not deducted initially from direct payments; they are reconciled after the annual assessment.

---

### 4.3 Unemployed / No Income (לא עובד)

- Receives the **minimum daily rate**: 328.76 NIS/day (2026).
- Payment is automatic from NII — no claim required.
- Students at higher education institutions also receive the minimum rate.
- Anyone whose monthly income was below the minimum threshold (9,863 NIS/month as of 2026) receives at least the minimum.

**Unemployed receiving unemployment benefits (דמי אבטלה):** Compensation will not be less than the daily unemployment benefit they would have received.

---

### 4.4 Part-Time Worker / Hourly/Daily Worker

- If worked **75+ days** in the 3 months before service: employer handles claim normally.
- If worked **less than 75 days** in the 3 months before service: employee must file a personal claim (Form 502) with NII, attaching employment confirmation and 6 months of pay stubs.
- Income from partial months is still included in the 3-month calculation; months with income below the minimum threshold are counted at the minimum level.

---

### 4.5 Extended Wartime Service (צו 8, 60+ days)

Reservists serving extended periods under emergency order are eligible for multiple layered benefits:

**Compensation stack for 100-day combat service example (2025):**

| Component | Calculation | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Core NII compensation (15K/month salary) | 500 NIS/day × ~101.4 adjusted days | ~50,700 NIS |
| Special Compensation (days 32–60) | 29 × 133.33 | 3,867 NIS |
| Special Compensation (days 61–100 under צו 8) | 40 × 133.33 | 5,333 NIS |
| Enhanced Personal Expenses (combat, from day 40) | 6 blocks × 466 | 2,796 NIS |
| Enhanced Family Grant (combat, with child, from day 40) | 6 blocks × 833 | 4,998 NIS |
| Additional Compensation (100 days) | ~2 credit points max | ~5,808 NIS (gross, less 25% tax) |
| Tax Credit Points (2026, combat, 100 days) | 3.5 points | 10,164 NIS (tax reduction) |
| Digital Wallet Grant | 21×30 + 69×80 = 6,150 → capped | 5,000 NIS |
| **Approximate total additional benefits** | | **~33,600+ NIS** |

⚠️ This is illustrative only. Actual amounts depend on individual circumstances, role classification, and policy updates.

---

### 4.6 Recently Discharged from Regular/Career Service

- If discharged from career service (קבע) and less than 60 days passed before reserve duty: compensation is based on last career service salary for 90 days.
- After 90 days: reverts to minimum rate (328.76 NIS/day in 2026).
- **2025 amendment:** Even if there was a break between reserve service periods, as long as the break was less than 60 days, compensation is based on the career service salary. Retroactive adjustments from the start of the war will be paid from June 2025.

---

### 4.7 Reservists Working/Living Abroad

- Those working for an Israeli employer with an Israeli payroll file: receive salary from employer as usual.
- Those NOT working for an Israeli employer: receive the minimum rate. Must fill out Form 912 on the NII website.
- 2025 minimum for abroad workers: 321.07 NIS/day, 9,632 NIS/month.
- Can apply to the Aid Fund (קרן הסיוע) for supplementary compensation for income loss beyond the minimum, up to the maximum rate (1,689.83 NIS/day / 50,695 NIS/month for 2025).

## 5. Employer Obligations (חובות המעסיק)

### What the Employer Must Do

1. **Continue paying regular salary** to the reservist employee as if they were working. The reserve compensation has the legal status of wages, and the employer must pay it on the regular payday.
2. **May not fire or discriminate** against an employee due to reserve service (protected by law; violations reported to the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission).
3. **Continue social contributions** — pension, provident fund, training fund — during the reserve service period.
4. **Submit reimbursement claim** to NII after paying the employee.

### How the Employer is Reimbursed

- **Form 501** (תביעת מעסיק להחזרת תגמולי מילואים): Submit to NII branch where employer's account is managed. Can be submitted online via the WCF system or the NII online portal for small employers (up to 10 reservist employees).
- NII calculates the compensation based on the reported salary and adds cost-of-living adjustments.
- If the NII-calculated amount **exceeds** the salary the employer paid, the employer **must** pass the difference to the employee.
- **Social contribution reimbursement:** Employer receives an additional 20% of the daily wage × service days as reimbursement for social contributions (pension, training fund, NII). ⚠️ This provision is based on temporary regulations through 2025.

### Required Conditions for Reimbursement

- Employer must have reported monthly salary (Form 100) for at least one of the 3 months preceding service, at least 2 days before filing the claim.
- Claim must include start and end dates of service (actual payment based on IDF-reported dates).

### Deadlines

- Employers can submit monthly claims even while the employee is still serving (wartime provision).
- Standard limitation: claims should be filed promptly; no specific statutory deadline published, but delays may complicate processing.

## 6. How to Claim (איך לתבוע)

### For Salaried Employees

In most cases, **no action is required** by the employee. The employer pays salary and claims reimbursement from NII.

**Exceptions requiring a personal claim (Form 502):**
- Employee was on unpaid leave (חל"ת) for 60+ days when called to service
- Employer ceased operations, declared bankrupt, or died
- Hourly/daily worker who worked less than 75 days in 3 months preceding service
- Working for multiple employers (for the supplementary income claim)
- Salary increase claim after Amendment 253

### For Self-Employed

- **Automatic payment** in most cases if NII advance payments are current.
- If not received within 3 weeks of service end → file Form 502.

### For Unemployed/Students

- **Automatic payment** at minimum rate.
- No claim required.

### Required Documents Checklist

| Document | When Needed |
|---|---|
| Military service confirmation (אישור צבאי / 3010) | All personal claims |
| Form 502 (personal claim for reservist compensation) | Self-employed, unemployed, special cases |
| Form 501 (employer claim) | Employer reimbursement |
| Form 510 (employer salary confirmation) | If not included in Form 502 |
| 6 months of pay stubs | Personal claims by employees, multiple employer cases |
| Employment termination confirmation | If fired/resigned before service |
| IDF combat service confirmation | Tax credit point claims (Form 101, Section 16) |
| Form 912 | Reservists living abroad without Israeli employer |

### Submission Methods

- **Online:** btl.gov.il — personal area / online forms
- **NII branch:** In person at the relevant branch
- **Employer online system:** WCF system or NII online portal for small employers
- **Phone:** NII call center *6050; IDF Miluim center 1111 ext. 4

### Key Deadlines

- Employer claims: Submit after paying the employee; monthly submission available during wartime
- Self-employed personal claims: If automatic payment not received within 3 weeks
- Tax credit points: Via Form 101 at start of tax year, or retroactive request within 6 years
- Aid Fund requests: Various deadlines; check miluim.idf.il (some through Dec 31, 2026)

## 7. Tax Implications

### Is Compensation Taxable?

**Core reservist compensation (תגמול מילואים):** Yes — treated as regular income. Subject to income tax, NII contributions, and health insurance contributions. Deductions are taken automatically (for employees via payroll; for self-employed reconciled with annual assessment).

**Additional Compensation (תגמול נוסף):** Not considered income for NII or health insurance purposes. Subject to a special flat tax rate of **25%** — no deductions, credits, or exemptions allowed.

**Special Compensation (תגמול מיוחד):** Paid net — typically no additional tax. ⚠️ Verify current treatment with tax advisor.

**Wartime grants (MOD/IDF grants):** Generally paid net without tax deduction directly to bank accounts. Most are not considered income for income tax, NII, or health insurance purposes.

### Tax Credits for Combat Service (from 2026)

As detailed in Section 3.10, combat reservists receive income tax credit points that directly reduce tax liability. Value per point: 2,904 NIS/year (2026). These are **in addition to** the standard 2.25 credit points every Israeli resident receives.

### Effect on NII Contributions

- During reserve service, NII contributions continue to be deducted from the compensation (for employees, by the employer; for self-employed, reconciled annually).
- Reserve compensation is included in the NII contribution calculation base.
- For self-employed: if NII contributions cannot be deducted from initial payments, they are settled during the annual reconciliation after the tax assessment.

### Reliability Notes

⚠️ **Rates change annually** — The 2026 rates (328.76 min / 1,730.33 max per day) are confirmed from NII and Kol Zchut. Always verify current rates at btl.gov.il.

⚠️ **Wartime provisions are mostly temporary** — Many grants and enhanced benefits are based on government decisions and temporary regulations (הוראות שעה) that may expire or change. The employer social contribution reimbursement was temporary through 2025; its extension is pending.

⚠️ **2026 benefits framework** — A 6.2 billion NIS framework for 2026 was reported in January 2026, introducing a 6-tier activity model. This framework is subject to Knesset budget approval. Details may change.

⚠️ **Additional Compensation (תגמול נוסף) exact table** — The per-day breakdown of credit point multipliers varies and was not fully available in the researched sources. The maximum is 2 credit points. Check the IDF Miluim website (miluim.idf.il) for the exact current table.

⚠️ **Digital Wallet Grant** — Implementation details (which services accept it, expiration) were still being defined as of research date.

⚠️ **Tax credit points require legislation** — The credit point system for combat reservists was enacted via Amendment 283 to the Income Tax Ordinance (2025). The 2028+ expanded eligibility (20 days) is already legislated.
