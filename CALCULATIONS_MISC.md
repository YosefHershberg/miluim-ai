## 6. Business Owner Compensation (פיצוי לבעלי עסקים)

**Eligibility:** Self-employed or controlling shareholders (25%+ ownership) called under צו 8 for 21+ days (Track 2) or 30+ days (Track 1), with declined business income.

**Paid by:** Tax Authority (רשות המסים).

**Formula:** Based on comparison of business turnover in the claim period vs. equivalent periods in 2022–2023. Two tracks for different periods in 2024.

**Supplementary aid:** Via Reservist Aid Fund (קרן הסיוע) for amounts beyond Tax Authority compensation, up to the maximum daily rate.

> Complex — consult a tax professional or use the Tax Authority online tools.

## 7. Employer Obligations & Reimbursement

### 7.1 Employer Social Contribution Reimbursement

**Formula:**
```
Daily_Reimbursement = 20% × (Employee's 3-month gross salary ÷ 90)
Total_Reimbursement = Daily_Reimbursement × Service_Days
```

**Eligibility:** Non-public-sector employers only. Compensates for pension, training fund, and NII payments continued during reserve service. Established April 2024; extension beyond 2025 pending.

### 7.2–7.4 Employer Process

1. Continue paying regular salary on regular payday (reserve compensation has wage status).
2. May not fire or discriminate against reservist employees.
3. Continue all social contributions during service.
4. Submit **Form 501** to NII (online via WCF system or NII portal for small employers).
5. If NII-calculated amount exceeds salary paid → must pass difference to employee.

## 8. Special Cases

### 8.1 Salaried Employee (שכיר)
Standard: employer pays → claims reimbursement from NII. Employee files **Form 502** if: on unpaid leave 60+ days, employer ceased operations, hourly worker with <75 days in prior 3 months, or multiple employers.

**Employee worked <60 days in 3 months:** Calculated on 3 highest-income months of the 6 preceding months.

**Multiple employers:** Main employer pays; employee files Form 502 for supplementary income. Total cannot exceed maximum daily rate.

### 8.2 Self-Employed (עצמאי)
NII pays automatically if advance payments (מקדמות) are current. Final reconciliation after annual tax assessment. If assessment shows higher income → receives difference + CPI adjustment. If lower → must repay.

### 8.3 Unemployed / No Income — Minimum rate: **328.76 NIS/day** (2026). Automatic, no claim required. Students at higher education also receive minimum rate.

### 8.4 Part-Time / Hourly Worker
- 75+ days in prior 3 months: employer handles normally.
- <75 days: employee files Form 502 with NII + 6 months of pay stubs.

### 8.5 Recently Discharged from Career Service (קבע)
If <60 days since discharge: compensation based on last career service salary for 90 days. After 90 days: reverts to minimum rate. 2025 amendment: applies retroactively from war start if gap <60 days.

### 8.6 Reservists Working/Living Abroad
- Israeli employer with Israeli payroll: receive salary normally.
- No Israeli employer: minimum rate. Fill **Form 912** on NII website.
- Can apply to Aid Fund for supplementary compensation up to the maximum rate.

## 9. How to Claim (איך לתבוע)

**Salaried employees:** In most cases, no action required. Exceptions → File Form 502.

**Self-employed:** Automatic if NII advance payments current. If not received within 3 weeks → file Form 502.

**Unemployed/Students:** Automatic at minimum rate.

### Required Documents

| Document | When |
|---|---|
| Military service confirmation (אישור צבאי / 3010) | All personal claims |
| Form 502 (personal claim) | Self-employed, unemployed, special cases |
| Form 501 (employer claim) | Employer reimbursement |
| 6 months pay stubs | Personal employee claims, multiple employer cases |
| IDF combat service confirmation | Tax credit claims (Form 101, Section 16) |
| Form 912 | Abroad workers without Israeli employer |

### Submission Methods
- **Online:** btl.gov.il — personal area
- **NII branch:** In person
- **Phone:** NII *6050 | IDF Miluim 1111 ext. 4

## 10. Tax Implications

**Core compensation (תגמול מילואים):** Yes — taxed as regular income. Subject to income tax, NII, and health insurance contributions.

**Calendar Grant (תגמול נוסף):** Not income for NII/health insurance. Subject to flat **25% tax** — no deductions or credits.

**Special Compensation (תגמול מיוחד):** Paid net — typically no additional tax.

**Wartime grants (MOD/IDF):** Generally paid net, not considered income for tax/NII/health insurance purposes.

**Credit point value (2026):** 2,904 NIS/year per point. Reduces income tax owed; cannot generate a refund beyond tax liability. In addition to standard 2.25 credit points every Israeli resident receives.
